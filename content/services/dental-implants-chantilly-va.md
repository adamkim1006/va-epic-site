## Why Patients Choose Dental Implants

Dental implants are often the most conservative long-term replacement option when a tooth cannot be saved. Instead of depending on neighboring teeth for support, the implant replaces the root structure directly in the jaw and supports a restoration from within the bone.

- Restore a single missing tooth without trimming adjacent teeth
- Replace multiple missing teeth with implant-supported crowns or bridges
- Improve denture stability when traditional removable teeth no longer feel secure
- Help preserve jawbone volume that is often lost after extractions

At VA EPIC, implant planning is prosthodontic-driven and diagnosis-first. That means Dr. Park evaluates the bite, restorative space, gum architecture, and bone support before deciding whether a straightforward implant, grafting, or a larger reconstruction is the better path.

## What We Evaluate Before Treatment

Every implant case starts with a candidacy review rather than a one-size-fits-all recommendation.

### Common factors we review

- Existing bone support and whether grafting may improve the long-term result
- Gum health and whether periodontal treatment should happen first
- The condition of nearby teeth and previous dental work
- Medical history, medications, and healing considerations
- Esthetic priorities in the smile zone

### Typical treatment sequence

| Phase | What Happens |
| --- | --- |
| Consultation | Exam, imaging, diagnosis, and treatment planning |
| Surgical phase | Implant placement with any needed grafting or site development |
| Healing | Bone integration and monitoring of tissue stability |
| Restorative phase | Abutment and crown placement once healing is appropriate |

Even when treatment is not immediate, clear sequencing usually makes the process feel much more manageable.

## When Implants Are Not the Only Answer

Some patients come in expecting an implant recommendation and instead learn that the better first step is periodontal treatment, extraction planning, ridge preservation, or a more comprehensive full-arch discussion.

That is a strength, not a setback. The goal is not to place an implant as quickly as possible. The goal is to recommend the option that has the strongest long-term prognosis for your smile, function, and maintenance.
